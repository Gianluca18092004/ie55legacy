Features

Internet Explorer 5.5 SP2 and Internet Tools works faster to save you time. From the IntelliSense™ features to the improved Search, History, and Favorites functions, Internet Explorer is simply easier to use. New features in Internet Explorer 5.5 SP2, such as Print Preview, give Web users more control; increased support for DHTML and CSS gives developers more options than ever. Learn more about the features of Internet Explorer:

Increased DHTML and CSS support
Expanded support for DHTML and CSS gives developers more control over the Web sites they build. With improved DHTML and CSS capability, you can:
  • Edit content and enter data through a WYSIWYG environment within the browser.
  • Build robust behavior components that encapsulate content, preventing specific data from inheriting unwanted properties.
  • Render body text vertically, in accordance with traditional Chinese and Japanese languages.
  • Develop sites that take greater advantage of frames, which can be layered.
  • Create pages that display menu items outside the browser window.
  • Incorporate advanced animation and graphic techniques into a page.
  • Use time-based code to control when elements appear on a page.
  • Customize the color of browser scrollbars.
Print Preview
New in Internet Explorer 5.5. Curious about how a Web page will look on paper? Print Preview will show you! With Print Preview, you can:
  • View each page as it will appear when printed.
  • Select only the specific pages you wish to print.
  • Zoom Web pages in and out.
IntelliSense
IntelliSense gives you automated features that save time when you're on the Web. Internet Explorer 5 has expanded automation in several areas, letting you:
  • Use AutoComplete to get a drop-down list of sites you have already visited or fill out forms automatically
  • Use AutoCorrect to automatically correct typos that would otherwise prevent you from going directly to the page you want
  • Use AutoSearch to quickly and easily find the pages you want
  • Use AutoInstall to automatically install Internet Explorer components when you come to a Web page that requires them
  • Use AutoDetect offline to get better visual clues about what items are unavailable when you’re working offline
  • Use AutoConfiguration to automatically locate and connect to the appropriate proxy server
AutoSearch
AutoSearch takes you exactly where you want to go. With AutoSearch, you can:

  • Type your query directly into the Address bar rather than having to open an additional window
  • Browse the results of your search in the left pane
  • View the page Internet Explorer suggests is the most likely match to your search
  • Receive all of this information in one easy-to-understand page
Related Links
Find new sites related to the one you're looking at, with an easy click of a button. With Related Links, you can:
  • Find new sites related to the one you're looking with an easy click of a button
  • View the list of related links as you browse through their pages so you don't lose track of where you are
Windows Radio Toolbar
The Windows Radio tool bar lets you tune in to your favorite radio station while you do your work. With the Radio toolbar, you can:
  • Listen to radio stations from around the world
  • Browse through the default categories of radio stations like News Radio, Classical, Country, and Modern Rock, and pick from the stations included there
  • Add your favorite radio station to the play list
E-mail
Outlook Express gives you more than one choice! With Outlook Express, you can:
  • Keep in touch with friends and family without losing sleep over costly phone bills.
  • Exchange critical information with colleagues so everyone in your business has up-to-the-minute information.
  • Mail greetings on designer stationery for those special occasions.
History
The History bar keeps track of where you've been by date, by site, by site visited most often, or by the order of sites you've visited today. The Internet Explorer's History bar lets you:
  • Find your way back to Web pages you viewed earlier in the day, the week, two weeks ago, or three weeks ago
  • View the history list alongside the links' pages so you don't have to keep clicking back and forth between the list and the pages themselves
  • View the list by site, by pages you visit most often, or by the order in which you visited the pages today
  • View a list that's alphabetized without the "www"
  • Search your history list for a specific page
Offline Browsing
Reading offline saves time and money by letting you read Web pages when you're not connected to the Internet. With Offline Browsing, you can:

  • View Web pages when you aren’t connected to the Internet
  • Save entire Web pages, including images, to browse at your convenience
  • Set a schedule for updating offline pages
  • Save connect time and money by scheduling downloads for off-peak hours
Web Accessories
You can customize Internet Explorer yourself, or download one of the tailored Web Accessories designed to take advantage of Internet Explorer's technology. With Web Accessories, you can:
  • Customize Internet Explorer by adding popular Web sites’ functionality to your browser
  • Expand Internet Explorer's functionality
  • Get a better, more innovative Web experience
  • Enjoy popular Web sites more by downloading their custom-designed Web accessories
Content Advisor
Worried about what sites your children might come across? Set limits on what they can see with the Content Advisor. With Content Advisor, you can:
  • Use the Parental Internet Content Selection (PICS) Rules to decide what your children can and cannot see when they browse
  • Use a trusted organization’s rating system to determine which sites your children can and cannot see
  • Specify approved and disapproved sites for your children, regardless of the way sites have rated themselves
Search Assistant
New and improved, the Search Assistant lets you choose your search engine and type of search! With the Search Assistant, you can:
  • Search for a type of information, including a Web page, an email address, a company or an organization, a map, encyclopedia articles, or for newsgroups
  • Choose which search engines you want Search Assistant to choose to use for each type of information you’re looking for, or use the one Search Assistant selects for you
  • Search your previous search results
  • Perform the same search with a different search engine
